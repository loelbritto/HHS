Bio-inspired Artificial Muscle - IIT Kanpur

Innovative Artificial Muscle Technology

The Smart Materials, Structures and Systems (SMSS) Lab at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT), Kanpur, has developed a Bio-inspired Artificial Muscle for Next-Generation Space Robots and Medical Prostheses. The development was encouraged by industry demand and growth in the field of miniature, light weight, non-magnetic gear-free actuators.

Key Innovation

The team expanded the design space of the shape memory alloy (SMA) actuator by leveraging the characteristics of bi-pennate muscle architecture with about 70% enhanced muscle force output per unit weight.

Shape memory alloys are a class of smart materials that can restore their shape after being exposed to high temperatures. The customizable multi-stage hierarchy of the shape memory alloy-based bio-inspired muscle design will encourage researchers to develop adaptive robotic prostheses for bio-medical applications.

Research Team

  • Prof. Bishakh Bhattacharya, Professor, Department of Mechanical Engineering at IIT Kanpur
  • Kanhaiya Lal Chaurasiya, Senior Project Engineer, Mechanical Engineering, IIT Kanpur
  • A Sri Harsha, Project Engineer
  • Yashaswi Sinha, Project Engineer

Applications and Impact

The invention will facilitate the creation of a new class of Space Robots which will reinforce Indian Space Technology. It will be utilized for adaptive robotic prostheses for bio-medical applications in the country.

According to Prof. Abhay Karandikar, Director, IIT Kanpur: "Shape memory alloy (SMA) based actuators are considered better alternatives to the conventional actuators, although it has some limitations. Researchers at the SMSS Lab at IIT Kanpur worked upon those limitations and has developed this unique shape memory alloy-based bio-inspired muscle design that holds the potential to revitalize the Space Robotics and bio-medical technology industry."

Additional applications include:

  • HVAC flow control for Smart Buildings
  • Automobile applications
  • Maximizing Solar panel performance
  • Efficient Turbine design for Aircraft applications
  • Automatic control of aircraft wings

This technology will significantly reduce (one third of the current cost) the cost of bio-medical devices and increase the affordability of high performance systems like MRI Scanner, CT scanner and Surgical Robots.

Source: IIT Kanpur - Bio-inspired Artificial Muscle Developed at SMSS Lab

Shape Memory Alloy-Based Artificial Muscle Design

Research Overview

A recent study published in ScienceDirect focuses on the Finite Element Analysis (FEA) of a shape memory alloy (SMA) based parallel actuator mimicking the force enhancing multi-hierarchical multi-scale pennate muscle architecture. This research represents a significant advancement in artificial muscle technology with potential applications in robotics and medical prosthetics.

Bi-Pennate Muscle Architecture

The advantage of using pennate muscle architecture is that the fibers are obliquely inclined to the muscle line of action, allowing the fiber force to be coupled to macro-level muscle force, leading to five to six times higher force generation.

The study examined how SMA-based actuators can be designed to mimic the natural structure of human muscles, particularly focusing on pennate muscle architecture where muscle fibers are arranged at an angle to the direction of pull, creating a stronger mechanical advantage.

Methodology and Findings

The researchers used finite element analysis to model and simulate the performance of different SMA actuator designs. The focus was on creating a parallel actuator system that could provide enhanced force output while maintaining a compact form factor.

Key findings from the study include:

  • Confirmation that bi-pennate architecture provides significant force enhancement
  • Identification of optimal angles for SMA wire arrangement
  • Development of a mathematical model for predicting actuator performance
  • Validation of the model through physical prototyping and testing

The research demonstrated that by mimicking natural muscle architecture, artificial muscles could achieve significantly higher force output with lower energy requirements than conventional actuator designs.

Source: ScienceDirect - Shape Memory Alloy-Based Artificial Muscle Design
